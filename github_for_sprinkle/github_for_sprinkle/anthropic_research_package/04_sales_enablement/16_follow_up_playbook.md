## FOLLOW-UP PHILOSOPHY FOR ANTHROPIC

### Core Principles

**1. Value-First, Always**
- Every touch provides value (insight, research, introduction, relevant content)
- Never "just checking in" or "circling back" without substance
- Think: "What can I give them?" not "What can I get from them?"

**2. Evidence-Based Communication**
- Reference specific conversation points (proves you listened)
- Use data, customer examples, research findings (not generic claims)
- Cite sources, be transparent about limitations

**3. Respect Their Time & Culture**
- Concise emails (3-5 paragraphs maximum, scannable format)
- Long-term thinking (relationships measured in months/years, not days/weeks)
- Thoughtful, not transactional (mission-driven language, not quota-driven)

**4. Intellectual Honesty**
- Acknowledge when timing isn't right (give permission to defer)
- Admit what you don't know (builds trust)
- Transparent about your interests while aligning with theirs

**5. Multi-Touch Relationship Building**
- Plan 6-12 month relationship development (not single email â†’ sales call)
- Vary touch types (email, LinkedIn, content sharing, introductions, events)
- Stay relevant to their current initiatives (requires ongoing research)

### When to Adjust Cadence

**Increase Frequency If**:
- They're responding positively and engaging
- Specific opportunity is progressing
- They've requested ongoing communication
- Time-sensitive evaluation or decision in progress

**Decrease Frequency If**:
- Multiple touches with no response (after 3-4 attempts, space out significantly)
- They explicitly ask for less frequent contact
- Timing clearly isn't right (respect their signal)
- You're providing value but no immediate opportunity (shift to quarterly)

**Stop Completely If**:
- They explicitly say "not interested" (respect their decision)
- Unsubscribe request or ask to be removed (immediate compliance)
- Legal or compliance concerns (never push past boundaries)
- Clear misalignment that won't change (recognize it's not a fit)

## FINAL FOLLOW-UP PRINCIPLES

### The 7 Golden Rules

1. **Add Value First, Ask Second**
   - Every touch should give them something (insight, connection, resource)
   - "What can I provide?" before "What can I get?"

2. **Reference Specifics from Conversations**
   - Proves you listened, not just sending templates
   - Shows genuine engagement with their priorities

3. **Respect Their Time and Culture**
   - Concise emails (3-5 paragraphs maximum)
   - Evidence-based language (data, examples, research)
   - Thoughtful, not transactional (mission-driven framing)

4. **Think 6-12 Month Relationship**
   - Not single transaction or quick close
   - Strategic partnerships take time at Anthropic
   - Patience and consistency win over aggressive pursuit

5. **Be Okay with "No" or "Not Now"**
   - Long-term game (this quarter's "no" could be next year's "yes")
   - Respect their decision-making process and timing
   - Focus on relationship, not just opportunity

6. **Authenticity Over Persistence**
   - Genuine interest in their mission > frequent touches
   - Quality of engagement > quantity of emails
   - They can tell the difference between authentic and performative

7. **Make It About Them, Not You**
   - Their success, their priorities, their mission
   - Your value is in service of their goals
   - Partnership mindset, not vendor mindset
