### Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP)

**Framework**:
- **AI Safety Levels (ASL)**: Categorization of model capabilities and risks
- **Safety Gates**: Required safety measures before advancing to next level
- **Responsible Scaling Officer**: Jared Kaplan enforces compliance
- **Anonymous Reporting**: Employees can report safety concerns without retaliation

**ASL Levels** (Simplified):
- **ASL-1**: Current systems, minimal risk
- **ASL-2**: Systems that could be misused by non-experts (current Claude models)
- **ASL-3**: Systems that could enable experts to cause catastrophic harm
- **ASL-4**: Systems that could autonomously cause catastrophic harm

**Deployment Requirements**:
- Each ASL level has specific safety and security commitments
- Cannot deploy higher-level models without meeting requirements
- Transparent documentation of safety measures
- Third-party audits and evaluations

**Strategic Value**:
- Regulatory advantage as governments increase AI oversight
- Enterprise trust in safety-critical applications
- Differentiation from "move fast" competitors
- Brand strength in regulated industries

**Conversation Hook**: "Your Responsible Scaling Policy sets the standard for AI safety governance. Our platform aligns with those principles by [safety feature], ensuring enterprises can deploy with confidence that [risk] is mitigated through [mechanism]..."
